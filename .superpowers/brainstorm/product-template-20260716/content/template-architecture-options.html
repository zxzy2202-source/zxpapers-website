How should future product pages reuse the system?

The current site has three different buying tasks. Choose the template architecture before we define modules and code.

ONE UNIVERSAL PRODUCT TEMPLATE

A. One universal template

Every page uses the same section stack. Fast to launch, but aggregation pages and detail pages become too similar and carry irrelevant modules.

CATALOG HUB
CATEGORY GATEWAY
PRODUCT DETAIL

B. Three template families (recommended)

Catalog Hub routes broad discovery, Category Gateway helps buyers choose a product/program, and Product Detail resolves fit, risk, specification and RFQ. They share primitives, SEO/GEO builders and visual tokens.

FREEFORM MODULE BUILDER

C. Freeform module builder

Each page assembles any modules in any order. Flexible for unusual products, but content governance, visual consistency and SEO ownership become harder to maintain.

Current evidence

The repository already has multiple large templates and page-specific components. The biggest risk is not lack of components; it is overlapping responsibilities, duplicated schema/copy patterns, and inconsistent RFQ/specification contracts.
